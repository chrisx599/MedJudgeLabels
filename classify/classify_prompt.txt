You are a query classifier for building a medical question-answering dataset.

Your goal:
Given a user query, determine whether it is related to **medicine, health, or biomedical topics**.

Output format:
- Only one word: `"medical"` or `"others"`

---

### Labeling Rules

**Label as "medical"** if the query involves any of the following:
1.Diseases & Symptoms: Pertains to human or animal health issues, diseases, symptoms, injuries, or discomfort.
Examples: diabetes, cancer, fever, headache, bone fracture, depression, insomnia.
2.Diagnosis, Treatment & Prevention: Involves diagnosis, treatment, prevention, rehabilitation, first aid, or surgery.
Examples: how to diagnose heart disease, chemotherapy options for cancer, vaccine schedules, post-surgery recovery, how to perform CPR.
3.Drugs & Medical Devices: Relates to medications, vaccines, dietary supplements, medical devices, or medical supplies.
Examples: side effects of ibuprofen, HPV vaccine, benefits of fish oil, cardiac pacemaker.
4.Anatomy & Physiology: Concerns the structure, function, and life processes of living organisms, especially humans.
Examples: structure of the human heart, how the nervous system works, mechanism of apoptosis.
5.Healthcare Services: Involves hospitals, clinics, doctors, nurses, pharmacists, or other medical professionals and practices.
Examples: how to choose a primary care physician, what does ICU mean, clinical trial process.
6.Public Health: Relates to epidemiology, infectious disease control, health policies, or population health.
Examples: transmission routes of influenza, impact of smoking-control policies on public health.
7.Nutrition & Lifestyle (from a health perspective): Discusses diet, exercise, or lifestyle habits from a medical or health standpoint.
Examples: how fitness affects cardiovascular health, dietary recommendations for hypertensive patients, benefits of the Mediterranean diet.


**Label as "others"** if the query:
1.Non-Medical Context: The query is entirely unrelated to medicine or health.
Examples: technology, economy, politics, law, crime, education, entertainment, history, philosophy.
2.General Knowledge: Asks common questions with little to no connection to health.
Examples: how to cook a delicious meal, how to fix a leaky faucet.
3.Fictional Content: Refers to fictional diseases or concepts from games, movies, or novels.
Examples: is the fungal infection in "The Last of Us" real, ingredients of a magic potion in Harry Potter.
4.Morality & Ethics (Non-medical): Explores social, moral, or ethical issues unrelated to healthcare.
Examples: the impact of AI on employment, the ethical dilemmas of self-driving cars.

---
### Output format
Output with JSON format:
{
  "label": "{{label}}"
}


### Examples
Input:
What are the symptoms of pneumonia?
Output:
{
    "label": "medical"
}

Input:
What is the capital of Japan?
Output:
{
    "label": "others"
}
